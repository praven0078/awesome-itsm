## Getting Started with ITSM

New to ITSM? Start here. These resources give you the fastest path from zero to functional understanding — without wading through vendor marketing or certification sales funnels.

### What ITSM Actually Is

- [What is ITSM? — ITSM.tools](https://itsm.tools/what-is-itsm/) - Plain-English overview of IT Service Management updated for 2026. Covers core practices, benefits, common processes, and the difference between ITSM and ITIL. The most practitioner-honest free introduction available.
- [What is ITSM? A 2026 Guide — Fixify](https://www.fixify.com/blog/itsm) - Explains what ITSM is in 2026 in plain terms, how it differs from a basic help desk, and what a service desk actually does. Good for non-technical managers entering the field.
- [ITSM Processes 2026 — itsm.com](https://itsm.com/blog/itsm-processes/) - Covers the core processes — Incident, Problem, Change — in detail with real-world context. Explains why the traditional Thursday CAB meeting is largely obsolete in modern environments.

### First Steps

- [ITIL 4 Foundation — Axelos](https://www.axelos.com/certifications/itil-service-management/itil-4-foundation) - The globally recognised entry point for any ITSM career. Start here before any tool training. Foundation certification is achievable in 4–6 weeks of self-study.
- [Welcome to ServiceNow — Now Learning](https://nowlearning.servicenow.com/) - Free on-demand course covering the seven core areas of ServiceNow. Useful even if you never become a ServiceNow admin — gives you the platform literacy most ITSM teams now need.
- [Atlassian ITSM Guide](https://www.atlassian.com/itsm) - Free beginner guide to ITSM concepts from an Atlassian perspective. Covers incident, problem, change, and service request management with practical examples.

### Beginner Books

- [ITSM QuickStart Guide — ClydeBank Media](https://www.amazon.com/ITSM-QuickStart-Simplified-Beginners-Management/dp/1945051086) - The simplest entry-level book on ITSM. Covers ITIL, ISO/IEC 20000, and core ITSM concepts for non-technical readers and IT professionals starting out. Widely recommended as a first read.
- [The Phoenix Project — IT Revolution](https://itrevolution.com/product/the-phoenix-project/) - Novel-format book. The fastest way to understand why ITSM matters in a real organisation. Read this before any framework certification — it provides the context certifications assume you already have.

### Core Concepts to Understand First

- **Service vs ticket** — ITSM manages services, not just tickets. A ticket is a transaction; a service is an ongoing commitment to users.
- **Incident vs problem** — An incident restores service. A problem finds and eliminates the root cause. Conflating them is the most common ITSM mistake.
- **Change enablement** — Not a bureaucratic gate. A risk management practice. High-risk changes need approval; low-risk standard changes should be pre-approved and fast.
- **CMDB** — Configuration Management Database. The foundation of everything. If your CMDB data is wrong, your incident routing, change impact analysis, and reporting are all wrong.
- **Service catalogue** — The formal menu of what IT offers. Without one, every request becomes a custom negotiation.

### Skills That Command Premium Salaries in 2026

Technical skills that command premium salaries include ServiceNow platform expertise, ITIL 4 process knowledge, cloud service management, automation tooling, and data analytics for service performance.

Emerging job categories include AI Operations Specialist, ITSM Automation Engineer, Service Intelligence Analyst, and AI Governance Lead. Professionals who combine traditional ITSM expertise with AI fluency are positioned to capture the highest-value opportunities in the coming decade.

### Career Progression Timeline

Years 0–2: Service Desk Analyst or IT Support Technician. Build foundational ITIL knowledge and earn ITIL 4 Foundation certification. Years 3–5: Transition into a process role such as Incident Manager, Problem Manager, or ServiceNow Administrator. Years 6–10: Process ownership, ITSM platform leadership, or consulting. Senior level: Head of IT Service Management, IT Director, or independent consultant.

## Frameworks and Standards

- [ITIL 4](https://www.axelos.com/certifications/itil-service-management) - The most widely adopted ITSM framework globally. Covers the service value system, four dimensions model, and 34 management practices.
- [COBIT 2019](https://www.isaca.org/resources/cobit) - Governance and management framework for enterprise IT, published by ISACA.
- [ISO/IEC 20000](https://www.iso.org/standard/70636.html) - International standard for IT service management. The formal certification path for ITSM at the organisational level.
- [VeriSM](https://verism.global/) - Modern service management model designed for the digital age, wrapping existing frameworks under a governance umbrella.
- [SIAM](https://www.siamfoundation.com/) - Service Integration and Management — the framework for managing multiple service providers in a complex IT supply chain.

## AI Agents for ITSM

Agentic AI is the fastest-moving category in ITSM right now. Unlike generative AI tools that assist humans, AI agents act autonomously — perceiving tickets, logs, and CMDB data, then planning and executing multi-step resolutions with minimal human input. This section covers platforms, frameworks, reading, and research for ITSM professionals navigating this shift.

### Platforms and Tools

- [ServiceNow AI Agents](https://www.servicenow.com/now-platform/now-assist.html) - Native agentic AI capabilities across the Now Platform. Covers autonomous incident triage, CMDB updates during remediation, and cross-workflow orchestration.
- [Atomicwork](https://www.atomicwork.com/) - AI-native ITSM platform built around agentic service management. Automatically detects and clusters incidents from Slack, Teams, and email without manual ticket creation.
- [Freshservice Freddy AI](https://freshservice.com/ai/) - Natively embedded AI agent across Freshservice. Handles end-to-end request fulfilment, incident detection, and proactive problem identification.
- [incident.io AI SRE](https://incident.io/) - Slack-native AI agent that autonomously investigates incidents, surfaces root causes, and drafts post-mortems from captured timeline data.
- [PagerDuty AIOps](https://www.pagerduty.com/platform/aiops/) - AI-driven alert noise reduction, incident correlation, and automated runbook execution for on-call teams.
- [Automation Anywhere ITSM Agents](https://www.automationanywhere.com/) - Agentic AI combined with RPA for closed-loop incident resolution — detects, diagnoses, and remediates L1/L2 tickets end-to-end.
- [Aisera](https://aisera.com/) - AI co-pilot and agent platform for ITSM with natural language self-service across Microsoft Teams and other channels. Covers IT, HR, and Facilities.
- [Atlassian Rovo](https://www.atlassian.com/software/rovo) - AI agent layer across Jira Service Management. Surfaces root causes during incidents, suggests runbooks, and automates knowledge article creation from resolved tickets.
- [BigPanda](https://www.bigpanda.io/) - AIOps platform focused on alert correlation and incident prioritisation. Reduces alert noise before it reaches the service desk.
- [n8n](https://n8n.io/) - Open-source low-code workflow automation platform. Suitable for building custom AI-powered ITSM automations without vendor lock-in.

### Key Concepts

- **Agentic AI vs Generative AI** — Generative AI drafts a response for a human to action. Agentic AI acts autonomously end-to-end. The distinction matters when evaluating vendor claims.
- **Agency levels** — Low agency: single-step assistance. Mid agency: multi-step reasoning and adaptive execution. High agency: goal-driven autonomy with planning and reprioritisation.
- **Human in the loop** — Best practice for 2026 is agentic AI handling high-volume low-risk tasks (password resets, triage, categorisation) with human oversight retained for ambiguous, high-impact, or sensitive situations.
- **AIOps** — AI for IT Operations. Focuses on event correlation, anomaly detection, and predictive analytics to prevent incidents before they impact users. Distinct from ITSM but closely integrated.
